Excel automation & business dashboards

Turn messy spreadsheets into fast, reliable reports that update in one click.

Ideal for founders, managers and freelancers who live inside Excel all day.

Why this service matters

On major freelance platforms there are easily a thousand plus active Microsoft Excel projects at any time, many of them asking for dashboards, automation and cleanup rather than simple data entry.

At the same time, research on business spreadsheets shows that the majority of real‑world files contain errors in their formulas, which quietly distort reports and decisions if nobody audits them properly.

Spreadsheet errors vs. real decisions

  • Multiple studies on operational spreadsheets have found that a very high share of business workbooks contain at least one significant mistake, often because of copy‑paste formulas or changes made in a hurry.
  • Audits of real files used for reporting suggest that error rates of a few percent of all formula cells are common, and some older studies even reported that almost nine out of ten spreadsheets contained at least one serious issue.
  • Because dashboards often sit on top of these workbooks, a single wrong reference or sign can flip profit, stock or campaign numbers and send teams in the wrong direction.

Cleaning structure and automating calculations reduces the risk of silent errors and gives you more confidence that every refresh is showing a truthful picture of the business.

How The JARs handles Excel work

  • Audit before build: Review existing files, find hard‑coded numbers, inconsistent formulas and broken links before designing the final dashboard.
  • Separate input and output: Clear input sheets, lookup tables and assumptions on one side, and locked report sheets or dashboards on the other side.
  • Smart automation: Use formulas, pivot tables, Power Query and, where needed, simple macros so that refreshing with new data is a one‑step process.
  • Explainer notes: Short documentation directly inside the file so that a non‑technical client can understand what to change and what to leave untouched.

The goal is to make reports that you can safely hand over to a client, manager or investor without worrying that one accidental edit will break the whole model.

Typical freelance use‑cases

  • Sales, revenue or marketing dashboards that combine multiple raw files and show KPIs by day, region, channel or product line.
  • Finance and operations models that track cash runway, margins, inventory and key ratios with automatic charts and summaries.
  • Cleanup and restructuring of legacy spreadsheets so that formulas are consistent, easier to maintain and ready to scale to more data.

If you have sample files or a rough sketch of the dashboard you want, you can share them through the Contact page and get a practical suggestion on how to structure your Excel model.
